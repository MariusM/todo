## Challenges

- **npm cache EACCES permissions** — recurring environment issue hit in 3 of 4 stories (5.1, 5.2, 5.3). Managed via documented workaround (`--cache /tmp/npm-cache-fix`). Accepted as trade-off per Marius — workaround reliable, root-cause fix would be scope creep.
- **Pre-existing flaky test** — `queries.test.ts > sets updated_at to a valid timestamp on update` fails near UTC midnight due to timezone boundary. Known since Epic 4, not fixed. Low impact.
- **Docker daemon not available locally** — Dockerfiles couldn't be verified locally, relying on CI docker-build step for validation.
- **Previous retro action item missed** — project-context.md was not updated with Epic 4 accessibility patterns (assigned to Paige). Deemed not critical since project is now complete.

## Key Insights

1. **Story sequencing amplifies quality** — each story building on the previous one's foundation (tests -> e2e -> security -> deployment) made the whole greater than the sum of its parts
2. **Adversarial code review is the team's most valuable quality gate** — it matured from catching missing features (Epic 2) to catching architectural subtleties (Epic 5). The two-commit pattern (implement -> review fix) is an accepted, effective workflow.
3. **Overperforming on quality metrics creates confidence** — 98% coverage when 70% was required means this codebase is maintainable and refactorable
4. **Document-and-workaround is a valid strategy** for non-blocking environment issues during delivery sprints — not every tech debt item needs immediate resolution
5. **The entire product lifecycle completed cleanly** — 5 epics, 18 stories, zero production incidents, continuous improvement tracked through retrospectives
